## Philosophy & identity
- CONFIRMED — "Air Raid has been labeled to me just because of the guys I've coached under." Also: "To me, what you can get on this offense is a very efficient offense that's flexible. It's flexible to your personnel. ... if you've got a quarterback like Noah, you can grip it and rip it. If you don't, you can still win games and score points with a guy that maybe be a runner behind center." (arizonasports.com, Dec 13, 2024)
- CONFIRMED — At Marshall: "For me, there was really only one Air Raid guy at the end of the day, and that was Mike Leach... Everybody that's kind of broken off from him... has put their own flavor on it." (herald-dispatch.com, Aug 2024; exact wording per search excerpt — page itself rate-limited on fetch)
- SECONDHAND — Despite air-raid roots, his Marshall 2024 offense ran ~60% of the time (~201.7 rush ypg, 20th nationally); at Arizona he stresses balance so opponents "have to defend every part of the field" (balance quote CONFIRMED via search excerpt of tucson.com self-scout piece, 2025). (azdesertswarm.com, Oct 2025; arizonawildcats.com release, Dec 13, 2024)
- SECONDHAND — Doege emphasizes changing the offense's mentality, an idea he attributes to Leach's "mentality over everything" approach (paraphrase; exact wording could not be verified on-page). (247sports.com, spring 2025)

## Tempo
- CONFIRMED — Up-tempo, no-huddle is core: "Tempo can be a weapon, but it's only a weapon if we're doing it the right way, lining up fast..."; "The standard could be faster. We're trying to push it to extreme limits."; "If we can... execute it, then I think we can be really dangerous." (tucson.com, Mar 25, 2025)
- CONFIRMED — On influences: "Going to Ole Miss, learning the tempo mechanics of that system and how to run the ball — very explosive run game." (arizonasports.com, Dec 13, 2024)
- SECONDHAND — Tempo framed as an equalizer that wears defenses down by the third/fourth quarter (paraphrase). (azdesertswarm.com, Dec 14, 2024)

## Risk & aggression
- CONFIRMED — Head coach Brent Brennan (after the 2025 BYU game): Arizona has been "extremely aggressive this year on fourth down"; vs BYU "went for our fourth down six times, and we converted four." (azdesertswarm.com, Oct 2025)
- SECONDHAND — 2025 season: 18-of-27 on 4th down; reporters note more downfield aggression and balance than optics suggested. (azdesertswarm.com, Oct 2025)
- No public statements found from Doege himself specifically on deep-shot rate or 4th-down philosophy.

## Play-calling process
- SECONDHAND — No-huddle operation: sideline signals plus QB Noah Fifita hearing Doege via helmet comms until the 15-second cutoff of the play clock (paraphrase). (tucson.com, Mar 25, 2025)
- CONFIRMED — "The center is just an extension of the quarterback... he's gotta be very intelligent." (tucson.com, Mar 25, 2025)
- CONFIRMED — Year-2 delegation: "I feel like I can delegate some of the responsibility... Those guys know what I'm trying to get out of the scheme. Now, there's still going to be long nights because I'm a perfectionist when it comes to making sure the plan is exactly right for Noah." (tucson.com, Jul 3, 2026)
- No public statements found on booth-vs-sideline location or game scripting.

## Influences & tree
- CONFIRMED — "A major mentor to me was Graham Harrell, and he installed the system that I ran at USC for over three years." (arizonasports.com, Dec 13, 2024)
- SECONDHAND — Played QB for Leach at Texas Tech; coached under Leach disciple Mike Jinks (Bowling Green), Harrell (USC, Purdue), Lane Kiffin (Ole Miss). (azdesertswarm.com, Dec 14, 2024)

## Change since last stop & 2026 outlook
- SECONDHAND — Marshall 2024 (first play-calling job): 31.8 ppg, run-heavy; Arizona 2025: ~37 ppg, more pass volume, Fifita school-record 29 TD passes. (arizonawildcats.com, Dec 13, 2024; tucson.com, Feb 2026)
- CONFIRMED — Extended through 2028 ($1.1M/$1.6M/$1.7M). (tucson.com, Feb 2026)
- CONFIRMED — 2026: "I feel a lot more confident going into Year 2... We don't have to reinstall everything, it's just adding and subtracting and finding out what works." Also: "I want him [Fifita] to have an answer for every play." (tucson.com, Jul 3, 2026)
