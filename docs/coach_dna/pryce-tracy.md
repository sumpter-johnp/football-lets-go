# Pryce Tracy — Public Qualitative Record (CSU OC, 2026)

*BYU 2026 opponent: week 3. Play-caller identity verified in
`docs/play_caller_checklist.md` (Tier 2). **FIRST-TIME PLAY-CALLER — no
prior play-calling film exists; this qualitative record is the entire DNA
source for him.** Compiled 2026-07-15 from web research; every claim
carries its source and grade.*

## Philosophy & identity
- CONFIRMED — Tracy, mid-spring camp: "The basis of who we want to be is a run-first offense. We'll be able to do that with the backs we have, the tight ends, and we have the O-lineman and the quarterbacks." (csurams.com, 2026-04-07)
- CONFIRMED — WR Terrence Smith Jr. (played under Tracy at UConn): "our whole goal is to just get a bunch of explosives." (csurams.com, 2026-04-07). RB Oliver Lundberg: "I really think he uses everybody. He knows what kind of weapons he has." (same article) — players describe a distribution-heavy, personnel-matching approach.
- SECONDHAND — Collegian (2026-01-22, Michael Hovey): the offensive reset "starts with Pryce Tracy," with QB development the obvious priority carried over from UConn; hiring of Christian Pace as run-game coordinator signals a pass-efficiency/run-strength balance (paraphrase).

## Tempo
- No public statements found from Tracy on game tempo. A Collegian headline called the spring scrimmage a "high-paced offense" (collegian.com, 2026-04-13), but the article body attributes no tempo philosophy to Tracy, and Mora called the scrimmage playbook "vanilla" (same article) — treat as weak SECONDHAND. Mora's "We force fed 'em... We haven't held back" (gazette.com, 2026-03-31) refers to install volume, not game pace.

## Risk & aggression
- CONFIRMED — Mora, hire announcement: "He's a very aggressive and innovative mind, and he will be an aggressive play caller. He relates well to the players and helps bring out their best every day. I believe CSU fans are going to love the way he runs this offense." (csurams.com, 2026-01-01)
- Counterweight data point: Tracy's 2025 UConn passing game was extreme ball-security — Joe Fagnano 3,448 yds, 69%, 28 TD vs 1 INT (csurams.com bio) — suggesting "aggressive" may mean design, not turnover risk.

## Play-calling process
- FIRST-TIME caller, self-acknowledged. CONFIRMED — Tracy: "It's been awesome. It's been everything I've been working toward for 17 years... I've been very fortunate to be around a lot of really good offensive minds, a lot of really creative play callers. The problem is I probably have too many things with me, too many ideas... A guy like me just loves scheming and X's and O's." And: "We're in the lab right now, for sure, so we can test ideas, test thoughts, test different things." Also: "I'm cautiously optimistic... We have not arrived." (all csurams.com, 2026-04-07)
- CONFIRMED — QB coach Matt Mitchell (coached BYU's Bear Bachmeier in 2025): "Coach (Pryce Tracy) and I see the game very similarly. That's part of why we're together." (csurams.com, 2026-04-21). QB Hejny (paraphrase): coaches call plays to each QB's strengths.

## Influences & tree
- Résumé (CONFIRMED via csurams.com bio/release): Saban recruiting staff at Alabama (2011); Notre Dame 5 yrs under Brian Kelly (ST analyst, GA WRs, QB analyst); Idaho RBs (2019-21); Georgia 2022-24 as assistant to Kirby Smart / assistant TEs (Monken-then-Bobo offense era); UConn 2025 PGC/QBs under OC Gordon Sammis and Mora.
- SECONDHAND — CSU athletics writer names Mike Bobo as "one of the offensive minds Tracy has mentored under" (csurams.com, 2026-04-07).
- SECONDHAND — UConn 2025 Sammis scheme characterized as run-game emphasis with play-action and RPOs; Sammis coached under go-go creator Brennan Marion (247sports UConn preview, 2025, via search snippet — not independently fetched).
- No public statements found from Tracy explicitly crediting Sammis, Monken, or Bobo by name.
- **Brief note:** the closest proxy film for Tracy is UConn 2025 (Sammis
  calling, Tracy running the pass game) — which is ALSO the proxy film for
  TCU's Sammis (week 5). One ingest, two opponents.
