# Scott Satterfield (Cincinnati HC / play-caller) — Public Record

*BYU 2026 opponent: week 13. Play-caller identity verified in
`docs/play_caller_checklist.md` (Tier 2) — Satterfield calls; his OCs never
have. Compiled 2026-07-15 from web research; every claim carries its source
and grade.*

## Philosophy & identity
- At his UC introduction (Dec 5, 2022) — CONFIRMED: "Offensively, we want to be a multiple offense. We have to find ways to get the ball in the hands of the playmakers and remain physical upfront." Also: "We have to be able to run the football and throw the football," and "you have to be very multiple... you just can't line up and do the same thing over and over and over." (gobearcats.com transcript)
- Beat coverage framed his arrival as importing the Louisville/App State ground game (Louisville top-3 in ACC in every major rushing category his last two years) — SECONDHAND. RB Ryan Montgomery — CONFIRMED: watching Louisville film, "man, they are really running the ball." RB coach De'Rail Sims — CONFIRMED: "You have to take the five-yard gain, you got to take the two-yard gain." (WCPO, 2023)
- Mid-2025 self-description — CONFIRMED (Oct 14, 2025): "Offensively, we haven't run many plays, but when we have run plays, we're pretty good. I think we're second in the country in yards per play." (SI/All Bearcats)
- At 2026 Big 12 media days he said mobility at QB is a scheme constant — "we've always had a quarterback that's able to move around a little bit" — SECONDHAND (via search excerpt of underdogdynasty/cincyontheprowl coverage; exact wording not independently verified).

## Tempo
- No public statement committing to a tempo identity. At his intro (Dec 5, 2022) — CONFIRMED: "We have been done fast tempo before and spread people out. We have also gone three tight end sets." His 2025 offense was low-volume/high-efficiency by his own account (quote above), and he added — CONFIRMED (Oct 14, 2025): "Obviously we'd love to get more plays... more opportunities to have the ball."
- After the 2025 BYU loss (Nov 23, 2025) — CONFIRMED: "That's their plan, and they do a great job of huddling, slowing the game down, and handing the ball to LJ (Martin)" — BYU's ball-control directly squeezed his possession count in a 26-14 win. (Deseret News)

## Risk & aggression
- CONFIRMED (Sept 30, 2025, on fourth downs after the Kansas win): "I think in this league, you have to be aggressive, I really do... if you think you got a chance to get the first down, let's go ahead and get it," and "You have to be aggressive in your thinking." (gobearcats.com)

## Play-calling process
- CONFIRMED (Jan 11, 2023, SI/All Bearcats): "I plan on calling the plays, but I think some people make a big deal out of that more than they need to." "My whole career, calling plays has been very collaborative." "We're the offensive staff, and I want ownership by everybody in that room." He is "the one who puts out the last play." Ego framing — CONFIRMED: "you throw the egos out the window, and we're all in this thing together as a team."
- SI (2026) reports he enters his fourth season calling plays — SECONDHAND. On promoting the 2026 co-OCs (Jan 15, 2026) — CONFIRMED: on Pete Thomas, "His player development skills and field vision have not gone unnoticed"; on Nic Cardwell, "Our offensive line has set the tone for our team during my time here." No public statements found on the co-OCs' exact game-day division of labor.

## Influences & tree
- CONFIRMED (Dec 5, 2022): "My head coach, Jerry Moore, who was there a long time as a Hall of Fame coach and did things the right way. That's where I learned to do it like that." — played QB and coached ~15 years under Moore at App State; credited with the 2004 spread-option installation there — SECONDHAND (Wikipedia/bios).

## 2026 outlook
- Sorsby is GONE: transferred to Texas Tech in January 2026, then ruled out by NCAA gambling fallout; he will sit out 2026 entirely and prep for the 2027 draft — SECONDHAND (ESPN, June 15, 2026; NFL.com/CBS). BYU will face Georgia Southern transfer **JC French IV** — Satterfield, CONFIRMED (Big 12 media days, July 2026): "We tried to find a guy that fit what we wanted to do... he ended up being the all-time leading passer in Georgia Southern school history." French dominated spring first-team reps — SECONDHAND. Roster churn is heavy (30+ newcomers; zero returning skill-position starters — SECONDHAND). Tone-setting, CONFIRMED (July 8, 2026): "we've gotten better each and every year in this league."
