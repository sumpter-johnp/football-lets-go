# Jake Spavital — Public Record (Baylor OC 2024–26)

*BYU 2026 opponent: week 11. Play-caller identity verified in
`docs/play_caller_checklist.md` (Tier 2). Compiled 2026-07-15 from web
research; every claim carries its source and grade.*

## Philosophy & identity
- CONFIRMED (reporter, texasfootball.com, 8/22/2024): "He started out as a Leachean Air Raid disciple who spent most of his time in 10 or 11 personnel," "added more run game and extra tight ends at West Virginia," and says varied defensive fronts "forced the old Air Raid worshippers to mix in a couple of curve balls." Same piece: "What Spavital still believes in is tempo."
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, Baylor Lariat, 12/5/2023, at hiring): "I always try to take around 10 shots vertically down the field a game. That's like the goal that I'm always trying to hit." Reporter pairs this with downhill running and a quick game (paraphrase).
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, 247Sports, spring 2026): "You just try to get the best players you can. Then you figure out how to use them" and "Every year can look different. That's the fun part." Also on choice routes: "It's a tedious process at first. But once they understand how to find space and get open, it becomes really fun to watch."
- SECONDHAND (Baylor Lariat, 11/19/2024): 2024 identity leaned on the run opening the pass; WR Josh Cameron (CONFIRMED quote): "Whenever you have a run game that opens up your pass game, we're unstoppable, honestly." RB Bryson Washington set the program single-season rushing record (1,028 yds) in 2024 (SECONDHAND, AP via The Intelligencer, 11/2024).

## Tempo
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, 247Sports, spring 2026): "Our biggest weapon is tempo. We want to dictate the pace and make it an exciting style of football."
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, texasfootball.com, 8/22/2024): "Our guys figured out around practice no. 8 of the spring that tempo can keep those exotic looks defenses like Dave's love in the back pocket." Tempo is explicitly framed as an antidote to exotic/multiple defensive looks.

## Risk & aggression
- SECONDHAND (Baylor Lariat, 8/30/2025): Baylor went for it on 7 of 9 fourth downs vs. Auburn (4 conversions, 3 failures), including 4th-and-1 from its own 34 in the first quarter; column dubbed it "bold playcalling."
- CONFIRMED (Aranda, same game): "I thought that we had a really good read on what they were going to give us, so it gave us a clear view on what we had to dial up" — Aranda endorses, doesn't restrain, the aggression.
- The standing "10 vertical shots a game" target (above) is his own stated risk baseline.

## Play-calling process
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, texasfootball.com, 8/22/2024): "It is hard to call plays and be a head coach. I've been in that position before. I just try to be the best assistant coach I possibly can and not have things go to his desk." Spavital has full offensive control under the defense-focused Aranda.
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, Baylor Lariat, 3/27/2025): "Sawyer is getting very good with his checks. I'm giving him a ton of freedom right now" — heavy QB check/option responsibility at the line; QB Robertson (CONFIRMED, 11/19/2024): "Coach Spav just makes it so easy for me, as far as my checks, my options that I can get to."
- No public statements found detailing in-game call mechanics (booth vs. sideline, scripting).

## Influences & tree
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, FootballScoop Q&A, URL dated 3/5/2025): "I was very fortunate to be around some really good coaches - Coach Sumlin and Kliff (Kingsbury) and Dana (Holgorsen)."
- CONFIRMED (Spavital, ESPN, 2016, on Holgorsen ceding WVU play-calling): "It was flattering because the guy who taught you how to call an offense was asking me, giving me the keys."
- SECONDHAND (texasfootball.com, 8/22/2024): "Holgorsen says this is why Leach kicked most of them out of the club – they ran the ball too much."

## Evolution & 2026 outlook
- 2024→2025: CONFIRMED (Spavital, Baylor Lariat, 3/27/2025): "Year two is always so much better as a unit … we're far more advanced [than] where we were at this point last year"; "about 90%" of the air-raid playbook installed by week one of spring. 2025 result: six-game win streak; top-6 nationally in yards/game, yards/play, points/game over Oct–Nov (SECONDHAND, Baylor SID/Broyles nomination coverage).
- 2026 QB: Sawyer Robertson went undrafted (2026 draft); Florida transfer **DJ Lagway** is the projected starter (SECONDHAND, SI Baylor, 4/25/2026). Lagway (CONFIRMED): "Me and Coach Spav's relationship has been great, with his ability to let me be me, let me play freely" (Baylor Lariat, 3/29/2026).
- 2026 intentions: CONFIRMED (Spavital, Baylor Lariat, 3/29/2026): "It's a brand-new team. We got a lot of new O-linemen, a lot of new receivers, new quarterback"; "I don't know if we're going to be a gap scheme team, an outside zone team, an inside zone team yet. We're throwing it all at them"; "We're keeping things basic early" (247Sports, spring 2026). Run-scheme identity was still unsettled as of spring 2026; expect the system to be rebuilt around Lagway's mobility.
