# Tyler Roehl — Iowa State OC 2026: Public Qualitative Record

*BYU 2026 opponent: week 6. Play-caller identity verified in
`docs/play_caller_checklist.md` (Tier 2, LIKELY grade). NDSU play-by-play is
too thin for a statistical profile, so this qualitative record carries extra
weight. Compiled 2026-07-15 from web research; every claim carries its
source and grade.*

**Path verified (corrects earlier assumption of a gap):** NDSU OC/play-caller
2019–23 (under HC Matt Entz) → Iowa State assistant HC/RBs coach 2024 under
Matt Campbell → Detroit Lions TE coach 2025 under Dan Campbell → announced
as Iowa State OC Dec 22, 2025 (joined after the Lions' season) under new HC
Jimmy Rogers. First FBS play-calling job.

## Philosophy & identity
- Roehl's own system description, on leaving the Lions: "I've really had a system that we ran at North Dakota State: pro style, under center, multiple with motion, run the ball, passing efficiency." — CONFIRMED (Pride of Detroit, late Dec 2025).
- On 2026 ISU identity: "We want to go out there and throw haymakers... That's the identity." and "[I want to] be the most physical team in the nation... Not literally, but the mentality of starting a fight every single game and throwing haymakers. That's the identity and that's the foundation." — CONFIRMED (The Gazette/Cyclone Fanatic, Feb 2026 media availability).
- Rogers on hiring him: "His offenses are known for creativity, innovation and toughness... as a former defensive coordinator, I know how hard those offenses were to prepare for." — CONFIRMED (ISU release via 247Sports/Radio Iowa, Dec 22, 2025).
- Track record backs the run-heavy label: top-5 FCS rushing offense in 4 of 5 seasons as NDSU OC; led nation in rushing TDs three straight years; his 2024 ISU run game jumped 688 yards to 2,247 — SECONDHAND (school bios/On3).

## Tempo
No public statements found on tempo preference. Indirect evidence points away from tempo: his self-description is "pro style, under center, multiple with motion" (CONFIRMED, Pride of Detroit), and InForum characterized the NDSU offense as layering in pre-snap shifts, motions and trades as the season progresses — a huddle-up, presentation-based operation, not a pace-based one (SECONDHAND, InForum).

## Risk & aggression
- Aggression framed as explosives, not gambles: the "haymakers" quote above; 2019 NDSU scored 38 of 80 TDs on plays of 20+ yards, 15 of 50+ (SECONDHAND, NDSU/ISU bio). SI characterized him as wanting to "set the tone with toughness and aggressiveness" via the run game (SECONDHAND, SI, June 19, 2026).
- No public statements found on fourth-down/analytics risk appetite.

## Play-calling process
- "You do your due diligence from a coaching standpoint. Making sure you're watching everything you can watch but not having paralysis from analysis." — CONFIRMED (InForum, Jan 2, 2022).
- "It's putting our guys in the best position to be successful. To be extremely multiple." — CONFIRMED (same InForum piece), where he also detailed a two-QB package built around each QB's strengths.
- Post-Lions: he now has "a whole different element that I'm able to add to my playbook" — CONFIRMED (Pride of Detroit).

## Influences & tree
- Played fullback/RB at NDSU under Craig Bohl (2,512 career rushing yards; Seahawks UDFA 2009) — SECONDHAND (bios/Wikipedia).
- Chris Klieman pulled him from Moorhead (Minn.) HS back to the NDSU staff (paraphrase) — SECONDHAND (Grand Forks Herald).
- Made OC by Matt Entz (2019); then Matt Campbell (ISU 2024); credited Dan Campbell and Hank Fraley for changing how he views TE/OL play (paraphrase) — SECONDHAND via Pride of Detroit summary; his direct words: "I've learned a lot, a lot. My growth as a coach, as a teacher, understanding really a pro system." — CONFIRMED.

## Change since last stop & 2026 outlook
He is explicitly porting the NDSU template up, augmented by NFL exposure ("whole different element"). Spring 2026 messaging is monolithic physicality: "Get all those tools in line and demand one thing: And it's to play with physicality"; "It's pretty glaring if you don't (play that way). And that's the standard."; "I'm happy with where we're at in practice. I'm not satisfied for a second." — CONFIRMED (The Gazette, Apr 5–6, 2026). Rogers called spring the most physical he's been part of (paraphrase, FootballScoop, Jul 8, 2026). QB is dual-threat Arkansas State transfer Jaylen Raynor (SECONDHAND, SI, Jun 19, 2026). Expect run-first, under-center, motion-heavy, play-action-based ball; note he sells a CoachTube course on integrating FB/TE in the play-action game (coachtube.com, undated).
