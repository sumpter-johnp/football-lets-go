# Marcus Arroyo — Public Record (ASU OC, 2024–26)

*BYU 2026 opponent: week 9. Play-caller identity verified in
`docs/play_caller_checklist.md` (Tier 2). Compiled 2026-07-15 from web
research; every claim carries its source and grade.*

## Philosophy & identity
- Dillingham's stated hire criteria/vision (CONFIRMED, Arizona Sports, Jan 22, 2024): "I wanted a quarterback-driven guy. I wanted a guy who's very, very detailed in terms of route depths — a lot less freedom, a lot more structure" ... and the offense he wanted: "A run play-action football team that plays fast with pro-style rules. A pro-style offense that plays fast."
- Arroyo entered 2024 wanting an identity that is physical, explosive and efficient, with heavy emphasis on the "middle eight" (last 4:00 of the first half, first 4:00 of the second) and turnover margin (paraphrase — SECONDHAND, Arizona Sports, Dec 20, 2024). On program-building (CONFIRMED, same article): "Football teams are aircraft carriers, man. It takes a long time to turn them around."
- 2026 players describe the Dillingham/Arroyo system as "QB-friendly" with a "simplistic approach" that schemes receivers open (reporter characterization — SECONDHAND, SI, May 14, 2026).

## Tempo
- No public statements found from Arroyo specifically about tempo. The only on-record tempo framing is Dillingham's "pro-style offense that plays fast" (CONFIRMED, Arizona Sports, Jan 22, 2024).

## Risk & aggression
- On his 4th-down shot call in the 2024 Big 12 title game vs Iowa State (CONFIRMED, Arizona Sports, Dec 9, 2024): "Nah, let's take a shot" ... "It was crickets on the headphones when I called it" ... "(With) Skat, everyone in the country knows on short-yardage who you're going to give it to. We worked it all week." Dillingham: "Yeah, I had nothing to do with that. That was coach Arroyo" ... "I almost wanted to say, 'Just hand it to Skat, just hand it to Skat.'"
- Counterweight: before the Peach Bowl he cautioned against overcomplicating bowl game plans — keep it digestible, focused on efficiency (paraphrase — SECONDHAND, 247Sports presser coverage, Dec 2024).

## Play-calling process
- Arroyo has called plays since arrival (Dec 4, 2023 hire). Dillingham audibled in early 2024 (Texas Tech, Cincinnati) then stepped back (CONFIRMED, ESPN, Sept 5, 2025): "I was actually s---ty, so I stopped doing it as the year progressed" ... "My best play, in my mind, is not the best play for the football team, because of how [Arroyo] prepared them" ... his current rule: "Give advice but don't give plays."
- Dillingham on delegation (CONFIRMED, Arizona Sports, Dec 9, 2024): "He watches more film than me when it comes to offense right now." Arroyo on the dynamic (CONFIRMED, same): "We're both high-energy guys, ultra-competitive" ... "We communicate probably as effectively as I've imagined."
- 2026 shift: Dillingham reportedly confirmed he will work "much more closely" with Arroyo on offense, after collaborating hand-in-hand at the Sun Bowl (reporter characterization, no direct quote found — SECONDHAND, SI, May 14, 2026). **Attribution watch item:** if Dillingham's 2026 involvement deepens, the clean "Arroyo calls" attribution may blur — monitor in-season.

## Influences & tree
- Verified tree (Wikipedia/school bios): San Jose State QB (1998–2002); QBs coach/co-OC at SJSU under Dick Tomey; Wyoming OC 2009–10; Cal 2011–12; Southern Miss OC 2013 under Todd Monken; Bucs QBs coach 2014, becoming interim OC when Jeff Tedford stepped away for medical reasons (2014, not 2019 as sometimes stated); Oklahoma State RBs 2015–16; Oregon OC 2017–19 (developed Justin Herbert); UNLV HC 2020–22 (7-23, fired Nov 28, 2022); ~2023 sabbatical visiting NFL/college programs and redesigning his playbook (SECONDHAND, Y-Option, Mar 24, 2025); ASU OC/QBs 2024–present. Also a 2013 Falcons minority internship under Dirk Koetter (SECONDHAND, UNLV bio).
- No public statements found from Arroyo naming specific schematic influences.

## 2026 outlook
- Stayed at ASU after the Giants interviewed him for QBs coach (Feb 2026); Giants hired Brian Callahan instead (SECONDHAND, Big Blue View / ProFootballRumors / Devils in Detail, Feb 2026).
- Sam Leavitt did NOT go to the NFL — he entered the portal Jan 12, 2026 and transferred to LSU (SECONDHAND, Devils in Detail).
- Open four-man QB race (Cutter Boley, Mikey Keene, Jake Fette, Cam Dyer), unresolved after spring. Dillingham (CONFIRMED, Arizona Sports, Apr 21, 2026): "It's a good battle. I mean, it's definitely going to go into fall camp." Arroyo (CONFIRMED, same): "You hope you get (a veteran guy in Keene) and we got that. You hope that Jake comes in as a young freshman, and … you're going to have some bumps and bruises but we're going to see what we thought we saw in our evaluation." Media consensus leans Boley (SECONDHAND, Yahoo/Cronkite News, Apr 2026).
