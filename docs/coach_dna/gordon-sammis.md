# Gordon Sammis — TCU OC 2026: Public Qualitative Record

*BYU 2026 opponent: week 5. Play-caller identity verified in
`docs/play_caller_checklist.md` (Tier 2). Compiled 2026-07-15 from web
research; every claim carries its source and grade.*

## Philosophy & identity
- Sammis on his scheme: "I think it kind of lives in the world of a little pro-style. It's a pro-style offense that's been tweaked to fit the college game." — CONFIRMED (247Sports exclusive interview, published shortly after the Dec. 11, 2025 hire; https://247sports.com/college/tcu/article/tcu-horned-frogs-gordon-sammis-exclusive-interview-266606058/)
- Dykes on the fit with his air-raid history: "I like the balance between run and pass. I like the fact that they have productive runners, productive passers"; "I like the fact they don't turn the ball over very often"; "He's an offensive line coach that's a play caller. There are not too many of those in college football"; "He's a physical, tough disciplinarian, and that's what we wanted, and that's what we needed." — CONFIRMED (247Sports, post-spring 2026, exact date not shown on page; https://247sports.com/college/tcu/article/tcu-horned-frogs-sonny-dykes-gordon-sammis-288219517/)
- Beat framing: Sammis arrives with "a different blueprint" than Briles — UConn 2025 averaged ~445 yds/35 ppg and led FBS in fewest turnovers; spring coverage repeatedly described a physicality/toughness emphasis he installed, including in the WR room. — SECONDHAND (https://roundtable.io/sports/ncaa/tcu/news/tcu-sends-loaded-group-to-big-12-media-days, July 2026; https://www.si.com/college/tcu/football/tcu-football-spring-pratice-8-takeaways-receiver-room, spring 2026)

## Tempo
- Sammis: "We can play any tempo that we need to play. We can pretty much run the schemes that need to be run." — CONFIRMED (247Sports exclusive, Dec. 2025, URL above). Adaptability, not a fixed fast/slow identity, is the stated position. No other public tempo statements found.

## Risk & aggression
- Turnover avoidance is the clearest stated value. Sammis on UConn: "We had a quarterback that didn't put the ball in jeopardy"; on QB criteria: "A guy that is going to throw it to the right guy, a guy that's not going to turn it over." — CONFIRMED (247Sports exclusive, Dec. 2025). Context: UConn QB Joe Fagnano threw 28 TD vs 1 INT in 2025 — SECONDHAND (Footballscoop, Dec. 11, 2025; https://www.footballscoop.com/2025/12/11/sources-confirm-tcu-has-found-their-next-offensive-coordinator-in-uconn-offensive-play-caller-gordon-sammis)
- Aggression lives in the run game/OL: a rules-based system "giving them the opportunity to go out and play like their hair is on fire and get after people. We're going to attack." — CONFIRMED (same 247Sports exclusive)
- SI analysis (May 15, 2026) argues his real impact is subtraction — cutting sacks/TFLs/turnovers rather than adding explosiveness — SECONDHAND opinion (https://www.si.com/college/tcu/football/tcu-football-gordon-sammis-hidden-impact-2026). No public statements found on fourth-down aggressiveness.

## Play-calling process
- Dykes on Sammis's design process: "there's nothing that happens randomly. Everything has a reason. It's just well thought out, it's well conceived." — CONFIRMED (247Sports Dykes article, URL above). Beyond this, no public statements found describing his in-game mechanics (booth vs sideline, scripting).

## Influences & tree
- Career (SECONDHAND, bio/wiki record): OG at Virginia 2003-07; GA Virginia 2010; Columbia OL/TE 2013; VMI OL 2015 (tempo RPO offense); Lafayette OL 2017; William & Mary OL/run-game coordinator 2019-21; UConn OL 2022-23 under Jim Mora, OC/run-game coordinator 2024-25, interim HC for Fenway Bowl. Two-time Broyles nominee (Pete Thamel, Dec. 11, 2025; https://x.com/PeteThamel/status/1999222521221251583). Mora called him "an absolute rising superstar" — CONFIRMED quote fragment (UConn announcement coverage, Apr. 2024; https://uconnhuskies.com/news/2024/4/13/football-gordon-sammis-named-huskies-offensive-coordinator). No public statements found in which Sammis names specific schematic influences.

## Change since last stop & 2026 outlook
- Sammis at hire: "This is a program with a history of explosive, dominant offenses, and I cannot wait to take the challenge of meeting those expectations head on"; "...attacking our 2026 plan right away." — CONFIRMED (TCU release, Dec. 11, 2025; https://gofrogs.com/news/2025/12/11/football-sammis-tabbed-to-lead-frogs-offense)
- First Power 4 job — SECONDHAND (Footballscoop, Dec. 11, 2025). Dykes discovered him watching UConn on TV: "I thought, wow, I like what they're doing from a scheme perspective... this is the guy we need to hire." — CONFIRMED (247Sports Dykes article)
- Spring game read (SECONDHAND, SI, Apr. 18, 2026): heavy motion, rollouts, outside runs; "This team is going to run the ball" (reporter's words) (https://www.si.com/college/tcu/football/thoughts-observations-from-tcu-spring-game-2026). SI floated 150 rush yds/gm as the 2026 benchmark — SECONDHAND (https://www.si.com/college/tcu/football/tcu-football-one-stat-defining-2026-season). Dykes post-spring: "Even more comfortable, even more excited than I was before." — CONFIRMED (247Sports Dykes article)
