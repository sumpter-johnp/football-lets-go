# Andy Kotelnicki — Public Qualitative Record (compiled 2026-07-15)

*BYU 2026 opponent: week 12 (Kansas, associate HC / play-caller). Identity
verified in `docs/play_caller_checklist.md` (Tier 2); his 5-season stat base
(KU 2021-23 + PSU 2024-25) lands with the Aug 1 ingest.*

## Philosophy & identity
- CONFIRMED — "It's a multiple, pro style offense, that uses spread concepts and components... it's about putting players in a position to be successful." (On3 takeaways from his intro presser era, https://www.on3.com/teams/penn-state-nittany-lions/news/penn-state-oc-andy-kotelnicki-on-where-hell-call-games-from-his-philosophy-more-takeaways/, undated)
- CONFIRMED — The "DQ Blizzard" analogy for simplicity-disguised-as-complexity: "it's going to look like a messy piece of deliciousness, but fundamentally is composed of really just vanilla ice cream and your favorite candies... at the end of the day it's just two ingredients mixed together." (FootballScoop, https://www.footballscoop.com/2025/03/03/new-penn-state-offensive-coordinator-andy-kotelnicki-compares-his-offensive-philosophy-to-a-dq-blizzard, quote from Dec 2023 PSU introduction)
- CONFIRMED — "We'll do whatever it takes to move the football and score"; "Your imagination becomes the limitation that you have as long as you're not overextending individuals"; "We're trying to make real art." His stated goal on every snap is "to put stress on a defense." (SI, 2025-08-04, https://www.si.com/college/pennstate/football/andy-kotelnicki-seeks-to-make-real-art-in-year-2-at-penn-state)
- Note: the word "conflict" was not found in his own mouth; his recurring terms are "stress" and "distort" (his clinic talk is titled "Distorting the Defense with RPOs," https://coachtube.com/course/football/andy-kotelnicki-distorting-the-defense-with-rpos/14223543).

## Tempo
- CONFIRMED — "Being multiple with the play you run, the formation you run and the tempo you run. That's so important. But you can't become a jack of all trades and master of none." (2020 Coach and Coordinator podcast, via The Progress News, https://www.theprogressnews.com/what-you-need-to-know-about-new-penn-state-offensive-coordinator-andy-kotelnicki/article_26171c36-909f-11ee-918d-13dd33afc3ef.html)
- CONFIRMED — Six core plays mastered "out of any personnel grouping, any formation, any kind of motion, any kind of tempo... Repetition and consistency is not the enemy." (247Sports, 2021 KU camp, https://247sports.com/college/kansas/Article/Kansas-offensive-coordinator-Andy-Kotelnicki-talks-Jayhawks-offensive-approach-168905888/; same theme in KU Sports "six-headed dragon," 2021-08-08, https://www2.kusports.com/news/2021/aug/08/why-oc-andy-kotelnicki-views-ku-football-offense-6/)

## Risk & aggression
- CONFIRMED (player, not him) — Drew Allar: "He's really aggressive, but it's not reckless at all." (Fox Sports, 2024-08-31, https://www.foxsports.com/stories/college-football/drew-allar-andy-kotelnickis-intentionally-aggressive-offense-look-like-perfect-match; "intentionally aggressive" is the writer's framing)
- SECONDHAND — On trick plays, he stresses preparation cost: "any scheme that you run, any play that you call, there's an investment on that" (per Daily Collegian, https://www.psucollegian.com/football/penn-state-oc-andy-kotelnicki-finding-balance-between-vanilla-play-calling-creative-wrinkles/article_25a62922-cdf7-11ef-9d85-d7130fe82dc2.html). No direct fourth-down-doctrine statements from him found.

## Play-calling process
- CONFIRMED — Calls from the booth: "a sterile environment, where you can be up there and you can think and digest without any of the emotion." (On3, URL above)
- CONFIRMED — Collaborative call-sheet building; asks assistants "If you were the offensive coordinator, how would you plan out the first 15 plays?"; calls it "scripting for success"; "One man can't do it all." (Lawrence Journal-World, 2021-06-11, https://www2.ljworld.com/sports/ku-football/2021/jun/11/ku-football-oc-andy-kotelnicki-takes-collaborative/)
- CONFIRMED — "As a staff, between access to film and watching other games, you steal a lot of things that you think will complement what you're doing." (AP via The Intelligencer, 2024-12-27, https://www.theintelligencer.net/sports/top-sports/2024/12/penn-state-offensive-coordinator-kotelnickis-creativity-has-the-nittany-lions-humming-in-the-cfp/)

## Influences & tree
- SECONDHAND — With Leipold since 2013 at Wisconsin-Whitewater (two D-III titles), 41 wins at Buffalo, then Kansas; also overlapped at Wisconsin-River Falls. (CBS Sports, https://www.cbssports.com/college-football/news/the-kotelnicki-effect-has-worked-for-penn-state-so-far-but-offensive-guru-can-prove-worth-vs-ohio-state/; SI, 2026-02-04, https://www.si.com/college/kansas/football/kansas-lance-leipold-shares-thoughts-about-rehiring-andy-kotelnicki)
- CONFIRMED — Leipold: "Andy Kotelnicki will be a head coach someday and probably someday soon." (SI, 2026-02-04, URL above)

## Return to Kansas & 2026 outlook
- CONFIRMED — On returning despite HC ambitions: "It's a goal of mine... But I've learned that fit and timing and being in the right spot is the most important thing, not your title." On reuniting with Zebrowski: "It was like riding a bike... I think Jim brings out the best in me and I bring out the best in him." (KU Sports, 2026-02-04, https://www2.kusports.com/sports/college/football/2026/feb/04/kotelnicki-explains-why-he-returned-to-ku-as-associate-head-coach/)
- CONFIRMED — 2026 structure per Leipold: Lubick and Zebrowski contribute (red zone etc.), "But ultimate terminology, day-to-day things of where it's at, Andy will have the final say." (SI, 2026-02-04, URL above)
- CONFIRMED — On growth since leaving: "What you don't want to do, is go back and fall into the same routine. Because we all should be different people two years later, right?" (KCTV5, 2026-03-31/04-01, https://www.kctv5.com/2026/04/01/andy-kotelnicki-reacclimating-jayhawks-football-during-spring-practice/)
- CONFIRMED — Penn State 2025 lessons: "We probably didn't combat the noise well enough"; "we probably should have done a better job of reminding our team how close we were to not winning some of those games in that [2024] season." (On3 interview via SI, 2026-06-09, https://www.si.com/college/pennstate/football/andy-kotelnicki-says-penn-state-didnt-combat-the-noise-in-2025)
- CONFIRMED — QB verified unsettled as of spring/summer 2026: Cole Ballard, Isaiah Marshall, Chase Jenkins (Rice transfer) competing; no starter named. Kotelnicki on the QBs: "Those guys have done a fantastic job of having the desire to want to grow and improve, and they've been super critical of themselves." (KU Sports, 2026-04-03, https://www2.kusports.com/sports/college/football/2026/apr/03/ku-footballs-quarterback-competition-well-underway-during-spring-football/; Roundtable, https://roundtable.io/sports/ncaa/kansas/game-day/kansas-football-enters-2026-season-with-quarterback-battle-still-wide-open)
- CONFIRMED — 2026 evaluation posture: "every day's an evaluation... you're looking for the ones who can consistently do it." (Through the Phog, 2026, https://throughthephog.com/andy-kotelnicki-s-latest-quotes-should-get-kansas-fired-up-ahead-of-crucial-2026-slate-01kn09w6qwt6)
